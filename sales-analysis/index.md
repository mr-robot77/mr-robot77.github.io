## Overview

This project conducts an in-depth **sales data analysis** to uncover patterns across time, geography, and product lines. The goal was to provide a business with clear, data-driven recommendations based on historical sales records.

## Methodology

- **Time-Series Analysis**: Examined monthly and quarterly sales trends to identify seasonality and growth patterns using Pandas.
- **Geographic Visualization**: Used **GeoPandas** and Matplotlib to create choropleth maps showing regional sales performance across the US.
- **Product Analysis**: Ranked products and categories by total revenue and sales volume.
- **Business Insights**: Translated quantitative findings into concrete recommendations for inventory, marketing, and regional expansion.

## Key Findings

- Sales peak consistently in Q4 (October–December), driven by holiday demand.
- The West Coast (California) and the Northeast (New York) account for the largest share of total revenue.
- A small subset of products (top 10%) contributes over 60% of total revenue — a classic Pareto distribution.
- Certain lower-performing regions show strong growth potential based on year-over-year trend analysis.
